# Creative Brief

## Working Title
Biomech Retro Horror

## High Concept
A lost-feeling 1990s horror-action game set inside an engineered biomechanical complex that blends flesh, metal, ritual, and industrial cruelty. The world feels alive but not friendly. The architecture appears to observe the player.

## Player Fantasy
Move through a grotesque chamber network, survive hostile constructs, decode environmental clues, and descend deeper into a world that seems intentionally built to mock human perception and suffering.

## Target Feel
- oppressive
- slow-burning
- ceremonial
- alien
- tactile
- readable at retro scale

## Core Pillars
1. **Readable biomechanical horror**  
   Strong silhouettes and memorable forms first. Texture and detail support readability, not replace it.

2. **Environmental dread**  
   Rooms should feel authored and symbolic, not procedurally random.

3. **Diegetic interface**  
   HUD, menus, and dialogue windows feel part of the world.

4. **Measured pacing**  
   Exploration, unease, and encounter buildup matter as much as combat.

5. **Vertical-slice-first development**  
   One polished room/level beats ten unfinished mechanics.

## Scope Boundary for Phase 1
This phase is not a full game. It is a vertical slice demonstrating:
- movement
- one attack loop
- two enemy archetypes
- one miniboss
- one environmental text/lore system
- one finished room theme

## Tone Notes
This project is inspired by biomechanical horror traditions, but must be its own world. Do not imitate specific copyrighted works. Build a distinct visual vocabulary from broader motifs.
