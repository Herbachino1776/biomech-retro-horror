# Art Bible

## Visual Doctrine
The world must look engineered by an intelligence that understands anatomy and industry but values neither comfort nor clarity.

## Primary Motifs
- ribbing
- vertebral arches
- tendons as cables
- fused flesh-metal transitions
- sockets, vents, membranes
- embedded faces and eye-like forms
- altar platforms and specimen frames
- pressure vessels and fluid channels

## Palette Direction
Use a limited retro-friendly palette centered on:
- bone / ivory
- rust-brown
- oil-black
- dirty steel
- bruised purple-brown
- muted sickly green for selective accents

Avoid bright rainbow colors, neon cyberpunk saturation, or clean sterile sci-fi whites.

## Sprite Readability Rules
- silhouette first
- texture second
- weird detail third
- every sprite must read from gameplay distance
- limit micro-detail that becomes visual mush when scaled down

## Background Rules
Backgrounds should feel dense and claustrophobic without obscuring gameplay.
Use layers:
1. far architecture silhouettes
2. mid-detail biomechanical structure
3. foreground cables/pipes/ornaments

## UI Rules
- frames are grown/forged structures, not flat panels
- dialogue boxes should feel inset into the world
- status meters can resemble pressure gauges, sacs, pulse channels, or reservoir tubes
- button panels should look tactile and unsettling, not modern glossy

## Animation Rules
Favor weight, drag, restraint, and organic-mechanical tension.
Avoid cartoony squash/stretch unless a specific enemy needs it.
